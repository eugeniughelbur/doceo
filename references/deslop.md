# Deslop pass

Run this on every lesson's prose (HTML page and vault note) BEFORE rendering. Rewrite, don't delete: keep the meaning, drop the tell. Sources: Wikipedia's "Signs of AI writing" (WikiProject AI Cleanup) via blader/humanizer, plus doceo's own rules.

## Kill on sight

- Em/en dashes (— –). Zero in final text. Use a period, comma, ` - `, or parentheses.
- Significance inflation: "marks a pivotal moment", "plays a vital role", "crucial", "essential" as filler.
- Overused AI vocabulary: pivotal, landscape, tapestry, underscore, foster, delve, robust, leverage, seamless, holistic, game-changer, cutting-edge, unlock, supercharge, elevate.
- Copula avoidance: "serves as", "boasts", "features" where "is" or "has" works.
- Negative parallelism: "not just X, but Y", "it's not about X, it's about Y".
- Forced rule of three: triplets for rhythm instead of content ("a concept, a codebase, a task").
- False ranges: "from X to Y" where X and Y aren't on a scale.
- Vague attribution: "experts say", "studies show" with no named source.
- Filler: "in order to", "due to the fact that", "it's worth noting", "keep in mind".
- Hedging stacks: "could potentially", "might possibly".
- Signposting: "let's dive in", "here's the thing", "here's what you need to know".
- Fake-candid openers: "Honestly?", "Look,".
- Manufactured punchlines: three staccato fragments in a row. For. Dramatic. Effect.
- Aphorism formulas: "X is the Y of Z", "X becomes a trap".
- Generic upbeat endings: "the future is bright", "exciting times ahead".
- Collaborative artifacts: "I hope this helps", "let me know if".
- Emojis anywhere. Title Case Headings. Excessive **bold** (one bolded phrase per section, max).
- Sycophancy: "great question", "you're absolutely right".

## Write instead

- Plain verbs: is, has, does, breaks, sends.
- Specific over general: a number, a filename, a command beats an adjective.
- Variable sentence length. Contractions where natural.
- Active voice with a visible actor.
- If a sentence could appear in any AI blog post unchanged, rewrite or cut it.

## Final audit

Reread the finished lesson once, looking only for the patterns above. One hit = fix it before rendering. The lesson should read like a sharp colleague at a whiteboard wrote it, not a content pipeline.
